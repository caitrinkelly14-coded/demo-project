Predicting Healthcare Staff Attrition

ISOM 835: Predictive Analytics & Machine Learning

Project Overview

Turnover in the healthcare sector creates significant financial and operational burdens, directly impacting patient care. This project analyzes a dataset of 1,676 healthcare professionals across Maternity, Cardiology, and Neurology departments to predict the likelihood of employee attrition. By identifying at-risk employees early, HR can implement targeted retention strategies.

Key Business Questions

  • Retention Drivers: What are the top factors driving healthcare staff to leave (e.g., Overtime, Distance from Home, Monthly Income)?
  • Departmental Risk: Are staff in certain departments at a significantly higher risk of burnout and turnover?
  • Intervention Strategy: Can we accurately predict high-risk employees so HR can intervene with customized retention incentives before they resign?

Ethics & Interpretability

Why Explainability Matters: When dealing with human resources, algorithmic transparency is critical. Relying solely on a "black box" model for HR decisions can introduce hidden biases. By utilizing SHAP (Shapley Additive exPlanations) values, this project unpacks the model's predictions to ensure retention policies are fair, unbiased, and addressing the root causes of burnout rather than penalizing demographic factors.
